Crowdsourcing

Crowdsourcing characteristics

  • There is a clearly defined crowd
  • There exists a task with a clear goal
  • The recompense received by the crowd is clear
  • The crowdsourcer is clearly identified
  • The compensation received by the crowdsourcer is clearly defined
  • It is an online assigned process of participative type
  • It uses an open call of variable extent
  • It uses the internet

Crowdsourcing examples

  • Amazon Mechanical Turk
  • Clickworker
  • Mobileworks
  • Threadless
  • iStockphoto
  • InnoCentive

Topic description

  1. Extract data from Yahoo Finance
  2. Build a knowledge base of company and product mentions
  3. Allow our users to query it and obtain meaningful information

Data example 2

Title: Surfing central banks in a benign 'QE trap'

Content: Despite rumblings of dissent about the financial bubbles and iniquities associated with zero interest rates and money printing, 2013 is ending with a remarkable certainty among global investors that cheap money is around for the long haul ...

Challenges

  • How do we identify which paragraphs have mentions?
  • How do we correctly aggregate aliases? (i.e. Hewlett-Packard & HP)
  • How do we correctly aggregate product lines? (i.e. iPhone & iPhone 5s, iPad & new iPad)

First approach: use a predefined list of company names

  • Use a predefined list of companies and products
  • Crowdsource analysis only for text fragments that contain mentions

Rationale

  • Crowdsourcing every paragraph looking for mentions is expensive
  • Identifying company and product aliases is hard
  • Collections of relevant companies and products exist
